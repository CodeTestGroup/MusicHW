Mindmap

Year 7 Term 4

  • Articulation – whether the music is smooth (legato) or detached (staccato)
  • Cello – a string instrument
  • Double Bass – a low pitched string instrument
  • Harp – an instrument belonging to the string family
  • Tambourine – a percussion instrument
  • Tempo – the speed of the music
  • Timpani – large orchestral drums with are pitched
  • Viola – a string instrument, larger than a violin
  • Violin – a high pitched string instrument
  • Xylophone – a pitched percussion instrument with wooden bars.
  • Year 7 National Anthem
  • Bass clef – tells you to play in the bass (lower register)
  • Choir – a group of singers
  • Keyboard – an instrument with around 88 white and black keys
  • Legato – smooth articulation
  • Melody – the tune
  • National Anthem – a national song of a country, group of countries or a nation
  • Key signature – tells you which key you are to play in (how many sharps and flats)
  • Staccato – short and detached articulation
  • Time signature – tells you how many beats are in a bar
  • Treble clef – tells you to play in the treble (higher register)

Year 7 Term 5

  • Beethoven – Composer from Germany
  • Choir – a group of singers
  • Concert – a music performance opportunity
  • Keyboard – a pitched instrument with octaves of white and black keys.
  • Mozart – Classical composer from Austria
  • Orchestra – a large ensemble with four families
  • BBC Proms – a series of classical music concerts during the summer.
  • Structure – the way music is structured
  • Symphony – a large scale work with several movements which sometimes includes a choir
  • Violin – a high pitched string instrument
